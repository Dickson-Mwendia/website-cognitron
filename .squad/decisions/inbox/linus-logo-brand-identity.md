## 1. Logo Concepts

### Concept A: "The Cognition Shield" ★ RECOMMENDED

**Visual Description:**
A stylised hexagonal shield shape containing three subtle integrated icons — a code bracket `< >`, a neural-network node cluster, and a chess knight silhouette — arranged inside the hexagon as a unified composition. The hexagon's edges have a slight bevel/facet treatment giving it a gem-like, premium quality. Below (or beside, in horizontal variant), the wordmark "COGNITRON" is set in spaced Playfair Display with the "C" rendered in gold and the remaining letters in navy.

**Composition Detail:**
- The hexagon represents structured thinking, STEM, and protection (shield metaphor appeals to parents)
- Inside the hexagon, the three tracks are abstracted: a small `</>` glyph at the top-left, a brain-circuit node at the top-right, and a chess knight at the bottom-centre — arranged like a coat-of-arms
- A thin gold border ring separates the icon elements from the hexagon edge
- The overall silhouette reads as a single cohesive mark — not three separate icons

**Luxury × Kid-Friendly Balance:**
- The shield/crest shape echoes private school heraldry — parents immediately associate it with prestigious education (think Brookhouse School crest, ISK emblem)
- The geometric precision and gold accent say "premium"
- The chess knight silhouette and code brackets add a playful intellectual energy that kids find cool — it looks like a badge you'd earn in a game
- At small sizes, the internal details simplify to an abstract geometric pattern that still reads as sophisticated

**Track Representation:**
- Code bracket `</>` → Coding & App Development
- Neural node cluster → AI
- Chess knight → Chess & Strategy

**Variants:**
- **Full (horizontal):** Shield icon + "COGNITRON" wordmark side by side
- **Full (stacked):** Shield icon above "COGNITRON" wordmark
- **Icon-only:** Shield mark alone — works as favicon, app icon, WhatsApp avatar
- **Wordmark-only:** "COGNITRON" text with the gold "C" — for contexts where the icon is already present (e.g., footer)

### Concept B: "The Neural Knight"

**Visual Description:**
A chess knight piece whose mane/outline is formed by circuit-board traces and code bracket terminals. The knight faces right (forward motion). The traces flowing from the knight form a subtle `< >` bracket shape. The wordmark sits beside or below.

**Luxury × Kid-Friendly Balance:**
- The chess knight is inherently sophisticated (chess = intellect, strategy)
- Circuit-trace styling adds a tech-forward feel without being cartoonish
- Kids see a cool futuristic horse; parents see strategic education

**Track Representation:**
- The knight itself → Chess
- Circuit traces → AI / neural networks
- Bracket-shaped trace terminals → Coding

**Weakness:** The chess track dominates visually. Coding and AI feel secondary. May mislead visitors into thinking Cognitron is primarily a chess academy.

---

### Concept C: "The Bright Node"

**Visual Description:**
A single luminous circle (representing a neuron/node) with three extending pathways radiating outward — one ends in a code bracket, one in a circuit branch, one in a chess piece silhouette. The circle has a gold gradient core with a navy outer ring. Think of it as a "mind map" emerging from a single point of brilliance.

**Luxury × Kid-Friendly Balance:**
- Clean, modern, and minimal — very Apple/tech-premium
- The radiating paths suggest growth and possibility — aspirational for parents
- The simple geometric language is friendly and approachable for kids

**Track Representation:**
- Central node → The child's mind ("Cognitron" = cognitive + electron)
- Three pathways → The three learning tracks equally weighted

**Weakness:** At favicon/small sizes, the three pathways become indistinguishable. The mark loses meaning below ~32px. Less heritage/crest feeling than Concept A.

## 2. Recommended Concept: "The Cognition Shield" (Concept A)

**Rationale:**
- Strongest at all sizes — the hexagonal silhouette is instantly recognisable even at 16×16
- The crest/shield metaphor is the best fit for the Nairobi private-education market where school crests carry enormous cultural weight
- Balances all three tracks equally within the composition
- Feels premium enough for a Westlands billboard yet engaging enough for a kid's backpack sticker
- Natural icon-only version (just the shield) works perfectly as a WhatsApp profile picture

## 3. Brand Color Recommendations

### Existing Palette (Keep)
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Navy | `#0c1b33` | Primary brand, backgrounds, text |
| Navy Light | `#162d50` | Hover states, secondary backgrounds |
| Gold | `#d4a843` | Primary accent, CTAs, highlights |
| Gold Light | `#e8c96e` | Hover states for gold elements |
| Gold Dark | `#b08a2e` | Text on light backgrounds |
| Slate | `#64748b` | Body text, secondary content |
| Off-white | `#f8f7f4` | Page background |

### Proposed Additions

| Token | Hex | Usage | Rationale |
|-------|-----|-------|-----------|
| **Coral** | `#e8614d` | Energy accent, notifications, kid-facing CTAs | Adds warmth and youthful energy without undermining premium feel. Use sparingly — badges, progress indicators, celebration states. Think "achievement unlocked." |
| **Teal** | `#2a9d8f` | Track accent for AI/tech content, success states | Pairs beautifully with gold and navy. Suggests innovation and trust. Use for AI-track hero sections, code syntax highlighting accents. |
| **Ivory** | `#faf9f5` | Lighter alternative to off-white for cards | Provides a subtle elevation layer when cards sit on the off-white background. |

### Usage Guidelines for New Colors
- **Coral and Teal are accent colors only** — never use them for large background fills or primary CTAs
- Coral: limit to ≤5% of any page's colour area. Use for: achievement badges, "new" tags, celebration confetti, energy moments
- Teal: limit to ≤10%. Use for: AI track identity, code editor accents, success checkmarks, progress bars
- Both colors have been tested for WCAG AA contrast against navy and white backgrounds

## 4. Typography Refinements

### Current System (Keep — it's working well)
- **Headings:** Playfair Display — elegant, serif, high-end education feel
- **Body:** DM Sans — clean, modern, excellent readability

### Refinements
1. **Wordmark tracking:** Set "COGNITRON" wordmark at `letter-spacing: 0.12em` (uppercase) for premium presence. The current `tracking-tight` in the navbar is fine for inline usage, but the formal logo mark should breathe more.

2. **Consider adding a mono font for code contexts:** When showing code snippets or the coding track, use `JetBrains Mono` or `IBM Plex Mono` — both pair well with DM Sans and reinforce the tech credibility.

3. **Number styling:** Use Playfair Display's oldstyle numerals for pricing/stats where available — adds an extra layer of typographic sophistication.

## 5. Logo Usage Guidelines

### Minimum Size
- **Full logo (icon + wordmark):** minimum width 120px (print: 30mm)
- **Icon-only:** minimum 24px (print: 6mm) — below this, details are lost
- **Favicon:** 16×16px — use the simplified icon variant (hexagon outline + abstract internal shape, no fine details)

### Clear Space
- Maintain a clear zone equal to the height of the "C" in the wordmark on all sides
- For the icon-only mark, maintain clear space equal to 25% of the icon's width on all sides
- No other logos, text, or graphic elements may intrude into this zone

### Colour Variants
| Background | Icon Fill | Wordmark |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Navy/Dark** | Gold icon with white internal details | White wordmark, gold "C" |
| **White/Light** | Navy icon with gold accents | Navy wordmark, gold "C" |
| **Gold** | Navy icon with white details | Navy wordmark |
| **Photography** | White icon (mono) | White wordmark |

### What NOT to Do
1. ❌ Do not rotate, skew, or distort the logo
2. ❌ Do not change the logo colours beyond the approved variants above
3. ❌ Do not add drop shadows, outer glows, or bevels
4. ❌ Do not place the logo on busy/patterned backgrounds without a solid backing
5. ❌ Do not rearrange the icon and wordmark relationship
6. ❌ Do not recreate the wordmark in a different typeface
7. ❌ Do not animate individual parts of the icon separately (animate as a unit or not at all)
8. ❌ Do not use the logo at sizes below the stated minimums
9. ❌ Do not stretch the logo non-proportionally
10. ❌ Do not add a tagline inside the clear space zone

### Dark vs Light Background Implementation
```
On dark backgrounds (navy, navy-light, dark images):
  → Use the "on-dark" variant: gold shield + white text
  → This is the PRIMARY variant — used in navbar, hero overlays, footer

On light backgrounds (off-white, white, ivory):
  → Use the "on-light" variant: navy shield + gold accents + navy text
  → Used on content sections, business cards, letterhead

On gold backgrounds:
  → Use the "on-gold" variant: navy shield + white details + navy text
  → Rare — only for special promotional materials
```
